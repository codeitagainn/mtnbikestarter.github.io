Gear You Need

Quality Helmet

On trails you want a helmet that is lightweight, well ventilated, and comfortable. This Giro helmet fits all of those categories. Almost all helmets are single impact so if you take a bad spill, get a new one.

More Info
Hydration Pack

Dehydration on a course or trail will end your day. A hydration pack allows you to drink without needing to pause your ride, carry a heavy pack, and gives you wallet and key storage.

More Info
Bike Gloves

Gloves will give you better control of your handlebars with the added grip and also add an extra layer padding for shock absorbtion. Riding in cold weather? Gloves will also help keep your fingers warm.

Gear You Will Likely Want

Multi Tool

Multi tools allow you to make adjustments to your bike when you're out riding. You won't know you need this until it's too late, get out ahead of the trouble with this 16 part multi tool.

More Info
Seat Cover

Your bum will thank you. Manufacturers put more effort into component quality than seat comfort (thankfully), you'll probably want one of these a half hour into your first ride.

More Info
Bike Bell

Let people know you're around in a more pleasant manner than "Hey! Watch out!". If you ride trails with a normal crowd or doulbe your bike as an every day bike, you'll want this.

More Info
Bike Pump

Your tires will lose air over time and with use. A pump with a psi gauge to accurately inflate your tires will help you have a smooth ride. A mountain bike tire should usually be between 30-55 psi.

More Info
Bike Rack

If you don't have a pickup and don't want to get your car irreverisably dirt, get a bike rack. Check this fit guide to make sure this rack is compatible with your vehicle.

More Info
Bike Light

Even if you don't plan on night riding, front and rear bike lights are a really good thing to have. Riding blind at night is scary for anyone, but it can be avoided fairly easily.
