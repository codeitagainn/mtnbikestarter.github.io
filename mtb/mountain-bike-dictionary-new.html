Glossary

Knowledge is power

mountain biking terms

single track

single track

narrow trail designed for one rider at a time.

double track

double track

trail typically twice as wide as single track designed for two riders at the same time.

trailhead

trailhead

entrance to a trail system. Typically near where you park your car and get set up.

line

line

the route you take on not so clearly defined paths of mountain bike terrain.

bike geometry

bike geometry

this refers to bike fit. It takes frame, handlebars, seat level, tire size and other factors into consideration for how a bike will handle and what it’s main purpose should be. Appropriate bike geometries will vary for different people.

26" tires

26" tires

these tires are smaller and lighter, easier to maneuver and quick to react. However, they are not as great as the larger tires at handling rougher terrain. At one point these were the only sizes offered on mountain bikes.

27.5" tires

27.5" tires

evens out the positives and negatives of the 26" and 29" tires.

29" tires

29" tires

they can handle considerably more terrain and have excellent grip. They are heavier and require greater lead time when maneuvering.

corners

corners

turns on a bike trail.

berm

berm

a bermed corner is where the corner is built up higher on the outside of the corner than it is on the inside.

switchback

switchback

Where a trail will turn 180 degrees on a tight corner.

drop

drop

where a trail falls away sharply and your bike has to leave the surface you’re riding in order to continue onward.

double

double

jump with a gap in the middle that needs to be cleared.

tabletop

tabletop

jump with a filled in gap. These are less dangerous than doubles.

skinny

skinny

a thin wooden beem raised off the ground for bikers to ride accross.

bicycle component terms

bike kits

bike kits

composed of the components you see on the frame. Included in a complete mtb kit is your cassette, chain, shifter, derailleur, racket, fork.

shifter

shifter

sets your gear. You can downshift and upshift. If you downshift you move the gears lower and the bike is easier to ride. Upshift and you move the gear index up, which makes the bike more difficult to pedal going uphill, but if flat or downhill you will pick up speed.

derailleur

derailleur

you have front and rear derailleurs they are the mechanisms that shift the chains at the front and rear cogs.

fork

fork

front suspension implementation generally categorized by the amount of travel the suspension provides.

hub

hub

what your cogset attaches to. Your hub is the component attached to your wheel.

sprocket

sprocket

the circular component with teeth (cogs) your bike chain rolls over as you are pedaling. Most bikes will be comprised of a ~10 sprockets.

cogset

cogset

a complete set of sprockets that attach to the hub on the rear wheel. There are two types of corsets, freewheel, and casset (wiki links).

general terms

suspension

suspension

spring and shock absorbers that cushion your bike from rough terrain.

hardtail bike

hardtail bike

bike with only front suspension.

full suspension

full suspension

bike with front and rear suspension.

rigid

rigid

bike with no suspension

suspension travel

suspension travel

this is the length your suspension will compress and decompress when riding over terrain or landing a jump. travel options vary but generally fall between 80-220mm.

components

components

when referring to individual parts of a bike, they are referred to as components. the section below details more individual components.

frame

frame

main component of a bicycle, onto which wheels and other components are fitted.

sadle

sadle

another term for bike seat.

disc brakes

disc brakes

brakes that use the friction of pads against a disc that is attached to the wheel. most mountain bikes will use some form of disc brakes.

rim brakes

rim brakes

brakes that use friction of pads against the tire rim. not freqently seen on mountain bikes.

drop seat

drop seat

a bike seat post that can be raised or lowered mid ride.
